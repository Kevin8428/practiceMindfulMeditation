It decreases stress and anxiety

There’s a seemingly endless amount of research showing connections to mindfulness meditation and lower stress/anxiety. A great introductory read is in the Harvard Gazette, which highlights results of a 2011 study by Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH) in which 16 participants received MRI’s two weeks before and after completing an 8 week mindfulness meditaiton program. Britta Holzel, an author of the study and fellow at MGH wrote “ It is fascinating to see the brain’s plasticity and that, by practicing meditation, we can play an active role in changing the brain and can increase our well-being and quality of life.”

  • Harvard Gazette: Eight Weeks to a Better Brain
  • National Institute of Health: mindfulness meditation for generalized anxiety disorder.
  • Harvard Medicine: Mindfulness meditation may ease anxiety, mental stress
  • Psychology Today: How Does Meditation Reduce Anxiety at a Neural Level?
  • National Institute of Health: Neural correlates of mindfulness meditation-related anxiety relief

It Improves Mental Focus and Clarity

Increases focus. Researchers from MGH and Harvard Medical School released a study in 2011 in which participants completed an eight week mindfulness training program. After concluding Dr. Catherin Kerr, director of Translational Neuroscience at Brown University noted "Mindfulness meditation has been reported to enhance numerous mental abilities, including rapid memory recall."

  • Harvard Gazette: Meditation may help the brain reduce distractions
  • Wake Forest University: Mindfulness meditation improves cognition

It Improves Self Compassion

In June 2015 researchers from the of Psychology at Fielding Graduate University in Santa Barbara, and the Department of Educational Psychology at the University of Texas at Austin released a study titled “Self-Compassion and Body Dissasisfaction in Women: A randomized Conrolled Tirla of a Brief Meditation Intervention.” In this study, women were split into two groups. The first received three-week self-compassion meditation training, while the other remained a control group. Results for women practicing meditaiton showed "significantly greater reduction in body dissatisfaction, body shame and contingent self-worth based on appearance, as well as greater gains in self-compassion and body appreciation”

  • Fielding Graduate University: Self-Compassion and Body Dissatisfaction in Women
